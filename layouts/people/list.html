Adam Gordon-Fennell, Ph.D.

Principal Investigator — Assistant Professor, Department of Psychology, Rutgers University

Adam is a circuit neuroscientist interested in how the brain orchestrates reinforcement and motivation. He completed his Ph.D. at the University of Texas at Austin (advisor: Michela Marinelli), where he studied the lateral preoptic area and its regulation of VTA dopamine neurons. As a postdoctoral fellow with Garret Stuber at the University of Washington, he developed novel open-source head-fixed behavioral systems, characterized lateral hypothalamic GABA and glutamate regulation of striatum-wide dopamine signaling during consummatory behaviors, and mapped striatum-wide dopamine dynamics using multi-site fiber photometry and 2-photon imaging. He joins Rutgers University as an Assistant Professor in Fall 2026.
