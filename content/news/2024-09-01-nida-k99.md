title: "NIDA K99/R00 award to support lab research"
date: 2024-09-01
summary: "Adam Gordon-Fennell was awarded a NIDA K99/R00 Pathway to Independence Award (K99DA059612) to study LHA and dopamine circuit mechanisms of positive and negative reinforcement."
---

Adam Gordon-Fennell has been awarded a **NIDA K99/R00 Pathway to Independence Award** (K99DA059612, 2024–2029) to support his research on hypothalamic and dopamine circuit mechanisms of reinforcement.

The award will fund studies investigating how lateral hypothalamus (LHA) GABA and glutamate neuron subpopulations — and their downstream dopamine circuitry — regulate motivation for opioid reward, using 2-photon calcium imaging and multi-site fiber photometry to track neural dynamics across stages of opioid self-administration.

The K99 phase supports completion of postdoctoral training at the University of Washington, while the R00 phase will fund the first years of the independent lab at Rutgers University.
